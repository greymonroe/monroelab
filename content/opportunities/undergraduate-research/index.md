        The Monroe Lab provides research opportunities for undergraduate students interested in gaining hands-on experience in molecular genetics, plant genomics, and evolutionary biology.

        ---

        ## What to Expect

        Students typically begin with an **orientation period** to learn laboratory practices, safety procedures, and the expectations of working in a research environment. This phase helps new students get comfortable before taking on independent projects.

        Undergraduates enroll in **research credit (199 course)**. One unit of credit corresponds to approximately three hours per week in the lab. Most students start with two to three units per quarter.

        Students are encouraged to **attend lab meetings** and participate in lab activities. Lab meetings are a great way to learn about ongoing projects, practice scientific communication, and become part of the research community.

        ---

        ## What We Look For

        We value:

        - Enthusiasm and genuine curiosity about science
        - Comfort learning new techniques and approaches
        - Interest in molecular laboratory research
        - Reliability and attention to detail

        No prior research experience is required, but some relevant coursework in biology, genetics, or biochemistry is helpful.

        ---

        ## Typical Timeline

        - **Fall quarter** — Orientation period. Learn lab protocols, safety, and expectations.
        - **Winter and spring quarters** — Enroll in research credit (199 course) and take on a defined research project.
        - **Summer** — Students who have participated successfully may be eligible for **paid summer research positions**, depending on performance and funding availability.

        ---

        ## How to Apply

        Interested students should email [gmonroe@ucdavis.edu](mailto:gmonroe@ucdavis.edu) with:

        - A resume or CV
        - A brief description of your interests and why you want to join the lab
        - Relevant coursework or laboratory experience

        We review applications on a rolling basis and welcome inquiries at any time of year.
